Starting with honesty

Addressing it directly.

I know I have real work to do on how I show up, specifically my attitude and mood under pressure, and how I respond to negative feedback and stressful situations. I want to acknowledge and own my mistakes. Here is my plan moving forward to grow, learn, and rebuild trust with the team:

Pause before I react. Stop, assess, then respond instead of responding out of stress.
Lead by example on hard days. Everyone has tough days in a high-pressure environment. When I do, I focus on keeping my reactions and attitude from affecting the team, and on improving how I respond so I can help others stay out of that headspace too.
Communicate early when I'm stretched. Flag pressure before it shows up in my tone.
Rebuild trust over time. Consistency week over week is the only proof that counts.

My Journal & Response Toolkit. Nick set up a dedicated space in Notion for me to journal my thoughts and reactions, and build better responses to stress over time. This is how I am going to hold myself accountable to the commitments above.

A process redesign

The Web Build Lifecycle

I replaced our old 3-milestone process with a multi-milestone, sprint-based system. It adds cross-functional collaboration between Strategy, WDT, and Content, and gives leadership real-time visibility into web workload.

In 2026 I merged sprint cards with monthly web update cards, so everything tracks in one place. It's one of the most significant contributions I've made to the team.

These three initiatives, the Compendium, the plugin, and this lifecycle, are the core of my case. I'm not just billing hours, I'm building the systems the whole company can rely on to grow.
